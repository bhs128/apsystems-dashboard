CT Commissioning Sequence (Solar Era, Nov 2024)

Solar era NULLs: Staged VUE installation, not sensor removal or power loss. All NULLs in the solar era are concentrated in the first 28.5 hours of monitoring (Nov 9–10, 2024). The Solar Combiner Panel VUE came online first on Saturday afternoon. The Breaker Box VUE was connected ~25 hours later on Sunday, with individual CTs clipped on over a 3-hour window. Zero null events after Nov 10 — no evidence of power loss or sensor disconnection.
Old era (100A Panel): All 10 breaker CTs report 100% non-null for all 34,074 rows. Solar columns are 100% NULL (solar didn't exist yet). This is expected and correct.

Key Findings & Recommendations

FindingDetail
100A Panel legacy data Device 33555 was previously the 100A Panel (May 2021 → May 2022). 34,074 rows, 355 days, zero gaps. Same physical breaker positions as the current Breaker Box monitor. Provides ~1 year of pre-solar baseline consumption data. No 15MIN export available before June 2021 (1H only for Feb-May 2021).
30-month offline gap May 2022 → Nov 2024 (903.9 days). VUE was offline between the old 100A Panel era and the solar installation era. No data recovery possible.
1DAY / 1H / 15MIN Essentially 100% coverage when exports are merged. Only gaps are DST spring-forward transitions (1.2h each year). These are the useful resolutions for historical analysis.
1MIN data VUE exports are capped at ~10K rows (~7 days). Solar panel has 21.5% coverage; Breaker Box has 6.2%. Large multi-month gaps. Not usable for continuous historical analysis without API access.
1SEC data Each export covers only ~3 hours (~10.8K rows). Essentially point-in-time snapshots with <1% coverage. Only usable for spot-check analysis of specific moments.
Column transition Breaker Box exports changed from 19→36 columns around March 25, 2025. Post-transition exports contain Solar Combiner Panel data. Merging must handle both schemas and avoid double-counting solar data.
Solar panel coverage gap Solar Combiner Panel standalone exports only cover Nov 2024 → May 2025. After May 2025, solar data only available via the combined Breaker Box export (36-col format).
Significant overlaps Exports overlap extensively — many zip files cover the same date ranges. Deduplication by timestamp is essential when merging.
Total DB coverage 82,056 rows across two eras: 34,074 (old panel, May 2021 → May 2022) + 47,982 (solar era, Nov 2024 → Mar 2026). 15MIN offers the best balance of granularity and completeness.
